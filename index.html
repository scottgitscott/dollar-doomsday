Clock position set editorially. Updated periodically by the site author.

The Dollar Doomsday Clock is not a prediction. It is a position. Like the Bulletin of Atomic Scientists' nuclear Doomsday Clock, it measures — editorially — how close the United States dollar is to terminal debasement. Nine minutes is a considered assessment, not a prophecy.

The methodology draws on four indicators: the trajectory of M2 money supply expansion since 1971, the decline in real purchasing power since 1913, the ratio of federal debt to GDP relative to historical precedent, and the accelerating pace of international dedollarization. Each is measured against what history tells us about how monetary systems end.

At nine minutes, the assessment is this: the dollar is not dead. The mechanisms of its decline are, however, well-established and accelerating. The distance between "structural problem" and "systemic failure" has rarely been shorter. The clock does not judge. It observes.
